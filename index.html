Week 1

Week 1 Experience

On the first day of my internship journey, a mix of nervousness and genuine excitement washed over me as we walked through the halls of DepEd Sorsogon. We received a brief orientation regarding the rules and a walkthrough of the building from Ma'am Laarne, our OJT coordinator.

Afterwards, we were assigned to our respective departments. We were divided into pairs, and I was partnered with Arjelone. Together, we were assigned to the Accounting Office. As we arrived, the office was very busy, but when Ma'am Laarne introduced us as the new OJTs, everyone greeted us with a warm welcome.

Sir Paul Andy, the head of the department, welcomed us and gave a brief explanation of the workloads that would be assigned to us. His assistant, Sir Eiman, then showed us our workspace cubicle and introduced us to Ma'am Dothy, who instructed us on our very first task: encoding liquidation reports from different municipalities of Sorsogon.

By the end of the day, we had accomplished 75 liquidation reports from Juban. The day was filled with introductions and new faces, and I realized that stepping out of the classroom and becoming an intern at DepEd Sorsogon allowed me to see how things truly work in the real world.

On our second day, we attended the first mass of August. After that, we were assigned a new task: writing liquidation reports for the months of April to July. By the end of the day, I had completed 100 files. Although my hands felt numb from all the writing, the task was still enjoyable and manageable since it only required writing the school name and corresponding month.

In the following days, our tasks remained the same encoding and writing liquidation reports. Since our workspace had only one computer, Arjie and I scheduled our tasks accordingly. When I was encoding reports, Arjie focused on writing, and after lunch, we switched roles to balance the workload.

As the first week came to an end, I realized how valuable the experience had become. The routine taught me patience and teamwork, while also giving me insight into real office dynamics sharing responsibilities, adapting to limited resources, and supporting one another. Week one was both challenging and fulfilling, strengthening my excitement for the rest of my internship journey.

Week 2

Week 2 Experience

As week two of my internship journey began, I felt more confident and comfortable in my role. The nervousness of the first few days had slowly faded, replaced with a growing sense of familiarity with the tasks and the people around me.

The same tasks were given to us either encoding or writing liquidation reports from different municipalities. Each day, we consistently accomplished one sack of files, which was equivalent to 100 reports.

In the following days, the routine became smoother as I grew more accustomed to the workload. Although repetitive, the tasks taught me the importance of accuracy, consistency, and time management.

By the end of the second week, I realized that internships are not only about learning technical skills but also about building discipline and resilience. The daily accomplishment of 100 files was proof of our dedication and effort. Week two gave me a deeper appreciation of the value of hard work, and it motivated me to look forward to the challenges and lessons that the coming weeks would bring.

Week 3

Week 3 Experience

As week three of my internship journey began, Ma'am Noreen gave us a new task: preparing the payroll for transportation and meal allowances of division test monitors, which had to be finished before the week of September. The real challenge began when Sir Carlo also gave us another set of liquidation reports that needed to be encoded. Since we only had one computer, Arjie and I decided to divide the work while I focused on Ma'am Noreen's task, she worked on Sir Carlo's. After every twenty items we accomplished, we swapped tasks so that each of us could also work on the other assignment.

At first, the task was difficult because I had to search for amounts across different files. However, as the days passed, I became familiar with the documents, and the process gradually became easier. By the end of the week, I managed to complete all the files assigned to me.

Week three was a challenge. Balancing two major tasks with limited resources was not easy, but it taught me the importance of teamwork and resourcefulness. What seemed overwhelming at first became manageable through practice and cooperation. Completing all the files within the week gave me a sense of accomplishment and reminded me that challenges are opportunities to grow stronger and more capable.

Week 4

Week 4 Experience

As week four of my internship journey began, I was given a new task by Ma'am Mel, which involved working on the tracking slip and payroll salary. It was an easy task since I only needed to find and copy the check number from each slip, but I also had to be very careful in copying the numbers because they were important and needed to be accurate.

In the following days, Sir Carlos assigned us the same type of work encoding but this time it was liquidation reports for the feeding program of different schools in the municipalities of Sorsogon. Despite the workload, we managed to complete every task given to us by the end of the week.

Week four highlighted the importance of consistency and focus. Even though the tasks seemed repetitive, each report carried significance for the department's operations. I learned that efficiency comes not only from speed but also from accuracy and attention to detail, and this realization strengthened my commitment to doing every task with care.

Week 5

Week 5 Experience

As week five of my internship journey began, it was already the month of September. We were so happy to receive a t-shirt from DepEd, which was given to us to be worn during the first mass of the month. Along with the mass, we also celebrated the opening ceremony of National Teachers' Month. It was a new experience for me, and I was very glad to be able to attend such a meaningful event.

In the following days, we were given the same tasks encoding liquidation reports for Senior High per month and preparing reports for the feeding program. Despite the repetitive nature of the work, we consistently finished every task assigned to us.

Week five was memorable not only because of the tasks we accomplished but also because of the experiences outside the office, such as attending the mass and the National Teachers' Month ceremony. These moments reminded me that being part of DepEd is more than just paperwork it is also about celebrating education and honoring the people who dedicate their lives to it.

Week 6

Week 6 Experience

A new chapter of my internship journey began in week six, bringing unexpected changes and adjustments. As the week started, some changes took place unfortunately, Arjie and I were transferred to the Cash Department because new intern students from SSU had been assigned to the Accounting Office, and their courses were more aligned with that department. Ma'am Laarne decided to transfer us, and it was fine since the Cash Office did not have any interns at that time.

On our first day there, it felt like the very first day of our internship again full of introductions, new tasks, and skills we needed to learn. When we arrived at the Cash Office, Ma'am Rica, the head of the department, greeted us warmly and was very happy to have us there. Her kindness helped ease our nervousness. We greeted them back and introduced ourselves.

As the days passed, we slowly learned how the Cash Office operates. Ma'am Amy was the one assigning us tasks and orienting us on what to do, such as assisting clients in finding their paychecks. Sir Raul guided us on where to locate payroll files for clients who were inquiring, while Sir Jeff and Ma'am Rica often asked us to deliver their paperwork or important files to other offices.

Being transferred to a new department felt like starting over, but it gave me the chance to explore a different side of office work and gain new experiences. The smaller team environment allowed us to build closer connections with the staff, and their guidance helped us adjust quickly. This week reminded me that every change brings opportunities to grow, and I felt more confident knowing that I could handle new challenges with patience and determination.

Week 7

Week 7 Experience

As week seven of my internship began, it's the opening of the BIDA ANG GURO Bazaar 2025 in celebration of Teachers' Day. The bazaar showcased unique booths from 14 municipalities, each promoting their schools' products. This turned out to be my busiest week so far.

On the first day, I was assigned to prepare certificates for the winners of the best booths and for the pageant participants of the DepEd Mr. and Ms. Ambassadors. At the same time, I also sorted report files and receipts. The second day was filled with raffles and games, and I volunteered to assist the Supply Office in distributing prizes to raffle winners. On the final day of the bazaar, we prepared both certificates and prize money for the winners of the booth contest and the pageant.

In the days that followed, I was tasked with writing the names of clients scheduled to receive their loan checks. The workload increased as many clients came to the office to claim their new pay slips for the month. During this time, Ma'am Amy taught me how to issue receipts for clients paying for the National Teachers' Month Celebration â€“ Gala Night.

Although the week was packed with responsibilities, it was also one of the most rewarding experiences of my internship. Balancing event preparations with client services taught me the importance of staying organized under pressure. By the end of the week, I felt proud of my accomplishments and more confident in my ability to handle demanding situations.

Week 8

Week 8 Experience

As week eight of my internship began, Ma'am Rica assigned us a new task: encoding the list of MOOE and PS monitoring reports for December 2024 and for January to November 2025. To manage the workload efficiently, Arjie and I created a schedule. In the mornings, I focused on encoding while she handled other responsibilities such as delivering files to the SDS office, sorting documents, and assisting clients. In the afternoons, we switched roles so that she could continue the encoding while I took care of the remaining tasks.

In the following days, we maintained this arrangement while also assisting clients as usual. During this time, Ma'am Amy assigned me to issue receipts for clients paying for the Lihaktura event.

Week 9

Week 9 Experience

As week nine of my internship journey began, it was also a memorable experience as we joined the grand opening parade for the Kasanggayahan Festival 2025. After the parade, we returned to the office to continue the same tasks from week eight, which included encoding the MOOE and PS reports while assisting clients in claiming their trust or provident checks.

In the following days, I did more file sorting and also learned something new Sir Jeff taught me how to handle changes in signatories for some clients. This week was both memorable and productive.

Week 10

Week 10 Experience

As week ten of my internship journey began, we continued with the same routine in week eight, focusing on encoding the MOOE and PS reports. Alongside this, I assisted clients and helped deliver reports to the SDS and COA offices.

In the following days, Ma'am Rica assigned me to photocopy some reports and organize them properly. By the end of the week, we successfully accomplished the task of completing the MOOE and PS reports from 2024 to 2025.

Finishing the reports felt rewarding.

Week 11

Week 11 Experience

As week eleven of my internship journey began, we spent the entire week assisting Sir Raul in arranging pay slips and payroll for both senior high school and junior high school teachers. In addition to this, I delivered reports to the SDO and Accounting offices and helped clients with their inquiries.

Later in the week, Sir Raul assigned me the task of writing down the details of clients who were scheduled to receive their trust or provident slips.

Week 12

Week 12 Experience

As my last week of internship began, we attended the mass for the Nazarene. After that, we continued with our usual routine of assisting clients in finding their pay slips, helping others with their trust or provident checks, and delivering reports to the SDO office.

On the final day of my internship at the DepEd School Division Office, Sir Jomar, and Sir Lance who were our coordinators, prepared a small gathering where we shared meals and received our certificates for completing our OJT hours. We also had the chance to take photos with the division heads, making the moment even more special.

Completing my OJT at the DepEd School Division Office was a meaningful journey from start to finish. I am grateful for the guidance, lessons, and experiences that helped me grow in both skills and confidence, and I will carry these learnings with me into my future career.
